Success Story

The research team successfully identified, analyzed, and find the best suitable tribal village in Chhattisgarh for proceeding ahead with the integrated Tribe Connect project. Villagers residing in the village discussed their problems with the research team and shown eagerness to adopt new technology for their Sustainable development and implement the Internet of Things and Artificial Intelligence for growth.

FINDINGS OF RESEARCH TEAM

Fulkarra is a small tribal village situated in Gariyaband Tehsil, Chhattisgarh.108 kilometers south of state capital Raipur. As per the government report, the total number population of Fulkarra village is 1898 which consists of 480 families and the total number of Scheduled tribe population in this region is 1100. On comparing the sex ratio of the village, the number of male candidates is 935 and the number of Female candidates of this village is 963. Fulkarra is a village is utilizing 4 G LTE network connectivity and 50% of the total population of this village are aware of internet connectivity and are familiar with smartphones. As a government policy of Chhattisgarh, to the inherent tribal village with the outside world, in 2018 government authorities have distributed 200 smartphones to the people in this village.

MAIN CHALLENGES OF VILLAGE

Healthcare Facilities in the village consist of only one Primary Healthcare Centre (PHC) which is having Doctors, Nurse, and Multi-Purpose Health Worker. Lack of modernized medical instrument and lack of technological advanced instruments, Doctor is only able to check primarily disease or symptoms of villagers as a result mostly they refer villages to the district hospitals for better care. The doctor is capable of providing only general medicines for Cold, Cough, and fever, etc. PHC is equipped with medical apparatus for checking Hypertension, diabetes, and weight but the most prominent diseases in Fulkarra identified are Leprosies or Skin Infection, Tuberculosis (TB), also genetic diseases are Sickle Cell, along with all these diseases villages are having of seasonal Infections such as Pneumonia during the winter season, Diarrhea during Summer season, Malaria and Dengu during Monsoon season. In social and health care sector Malnutrition problem is one of the serious problems of this region as per health care reports, most of the village Children & Pregnant Women are suffering from malnutrition problem The Existing Aaganwadi is taking care of pregnant women like weight testing, Diet plan, Providing Iron and calcium tablets but required advanced instrument for efficient monitoring. On focusing education sector, in this village, the children are using traditional methods of education but require animated methods of learning or story resembling solutions for primary schools, middle school and high school subjects like Maths, Environment and English Communication requires more attractive methods of learning which is possible through smart classes but in this village, there are no smart classes but the population is willing to adapt to new learning techniques and enhancements. Economic Growth in tribal village Fulkara is mostly dependent mainly on agriculture, 90% of the total population is dependent on agriculture. Paddy is the main crop grown in this area, Farmers cultivate paddy crops from July to November and only a few farmers grow green leafy vegetables and pulses and in the other halves of the year, they are dependent on government schemes such as Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment. In the agriculture sector, farmers are dependent on bore wells or tube wells as the main sources of irrigation, other natural methods include monsoon rains, river water coming from Mahanadi and Pari. Tribal Department i.e. Integrated Tribal Development Agency (ITDA) Chhattisgarh has provided land and bore wells for cultivation for only limited farmers. Due to lack of proper management, Fertilizers are supplied by government agencies at the Mandi system at a lower price but due to lack of supply chain management, farmers prefer private dealers. In this region are paddy, gram, Mussoorie are preferred which are available in stock centers at subsidized prices but due to lack of stock and management of stock centers, most villagers are dependent on local vendors which sell at higher prices. The government is providing a market platform where farmers get minimum support Price but the market structure is not properly managed and coordinated. Villagers have lack of knowledge about rotational farming and cultivation of new crops, Urea, and other fertilizers are sprayed only by assumption, not by definite measurement, which leads to a nutritional imbalance in the soil, pesticides are also purchased from private suppliers. Other problems farmers are dealing with is lack of knowledge about growing other crops, animal intrusions spoil farming crops, they need a system that can detect foreign organisms entering the field, Serious rice crop diseases which are dangerous for crop yield such diseases like brown planthopper (Brown Mau) need early detection. The village lacks a platform for local talent Promotion, the communication with the outside world is limited

RESEARCH TEAM SUGGESTION/ DELIVERABLES THROUGH TRIBE CONNECT PLATFORM

After finding problems of villagers, following are the objectives are taken into consideration for the development prototype process.

  • Establishment of ICT-based smart Anganwadi Kendra for children and pregnant women malnutrition care.
  • Development of a mobile software application capable of detection of anemia in Pregnant Women and children.
  • Development of a mobile application, for a Telemedicine facility for primary health care and diagnosis of community.
  • Establishment of software application for Farming-Disease prevention.
  • Provision of IoT based instrument for timely irrigation information.
  • Provision of gadget for Well water quality monitoring.
  • Provision of disaster monitoring and alerting system by IoT connected devices.
  • Organization of ICT based network to promote education.
  • Development of ICT based E-Mandi platform for marketing the tribal products.
  • Software application for the provision of basic e-Governance facility.
  • Deployment of software application platform Local talent Promotion.
  • Established communication with the outside world.
